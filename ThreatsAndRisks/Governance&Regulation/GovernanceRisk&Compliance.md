# What I Learned — Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC)

## GRC Overview

- Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) is a holistic framework that integrates:
  - Governance
  - Enterprise Risk Management
  - Compliance
- It ensures the organisation:
  - Aligns security with business objectives
  - Manages risks effectively
  - Operates within legal and regulatory boundaries
- GRC strengthens the overall security posture and supports business resilience.

---

## Components of GRC

### Governance Component
- Sets organisational direction through:
  - Security strategies
  - Policies
  - Standards
  - Baselines
  - Frameworks
- Establishes monitoring and performance measurement methods.
- Ensures leadership oversight and accountability.

### Risk Management Component
- Identifies, assesses, and prioritises risks.
- Implements controls and mitigation strategies.
- Monitors and reports on risk status.
- Continuously refines the risk management process.

### Compliance Component
- Ensures adherence to:
  - Laws
  - Regulations
  - Industry standards
- Includes:
  - Compliance programs
  - Audits and assessments
  - Reporting to stakeholders

---

## How to Develop a GRC Program

1. Define Scope and Objectives
   - Determine what systems/processes are covered.
   - Set measurable goals (e.g., reduce cyber risk by a specific percentage).

2. Conduct Risk Assessment
   - Identify vulnerabilities and threats.
   - Prioritise risks.
   - Develop mitigation strategies.

3. Develop Policies and Procedures
   - Establish formal guidance (e.g., password policy, logging requirements).
   - Define secure operational practices.

4. Establish Governance Processes
   - Define roles and responsibilities.
   - Create oversight committees (e.g., security steering committee).
   - Ensure leadership involvement.

5. Implement Controls
   - Technical controls: Firewalls, IDS, IPS, SIEM.
   - Administrative controls: Policies, employee training.
   - Mitigate identified risks.

6. Monitor and Measure Performance
   - Track metrics and compliance levels.
   - Identify weaknesses and areas for improvement.

7. Continuous Improvement
   - Review program regularly.
   - Adjust based on incidents, evolving risks, and stakeholder feedback.

## GRC Example — Financial Sector

### Governance Activities
- Assign governance-level executives.
- Develop policies:
  - Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
  - Bank Secrecy Act compliance
  - Financial audits
  - Crisis management
  - Financial reporting controls

### Risk Management Activities
- Identify financial and cyber risks:
  - Fraudulent transactions
  - Phishing attacks
  - Stolen credentials
  - Fake ATM cards
- Define countermeasures.

### Compliance Activities
- Ensure compliance with:
  - PCI DSS
  - GLBA
- Implement secure practices:
  - SSL/TLS to prevent MITM attacks
  - Patch management
  - Security awareness training

---

## Key Takeaway

- Governance defines direction and oversight.
- Risk management identifies and mitigates threats.
- Compliance ensures legal and regulatory adherence.
- GRC integrates all three into a unified, structured security approach.

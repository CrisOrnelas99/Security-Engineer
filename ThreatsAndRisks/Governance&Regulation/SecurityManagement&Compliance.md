# What I Learned — Information Security Management, ISO 27001, and SOC 2

## Information Security (IS) Management

- IS management involves:
  - Planning, implementing, and maintaining security controls.
  - Protecting information assets from:
    - Unauthorised access
    - Disclosure
    - Alteration
    - Destruction
    - Disruption
- Core activities include:
  - Risk identification and assessment
  - Development of security controls and procedures
  - Incident response planning
  - Security awareness training

- Compliance means adhering to:
  - Legal requirements
  - Regulatory standards
  - Contractual obligations
  - Industry frameworks

---

## ISO/IEC 27001

- International standard for building and maintaining an Information Security Management System (ISMS).
- Developed by ISO and IEC.
- Focus: systematic management of information security risks.

### Core Components of ISO 27001

- Scope:
  - Defines boundaries of the ISMS.
  - Identifies covered assets and processes.

- Information Security Policy:
  - High-level security direction of the organisation.

- Risk Assessment:
  - Identify and evaluate risks to confidentiality, integrity, and availability (CIA).

- Risk Treatment:
  - Select and implement controls to reduce risks.

- Statement of Applicability (SoA):
  - Lists applicable controls and justifies exclusions.

- Internal Audit:
  - Periodic audits to evaluate ISMS effectiveness.

- Management Review:
  - Regular leadership review of ISMS performance.

### Key Requirements for Implementation

- Conduct gap analysis.
- Perform comprehensive risk assessment.
- Develop clear policies and procedures.
- Ensure leadership support and resource allocation.
- Monitor performance and continuously improve.

## Service Organization Control 2 (SOC 2)

- Developed by the American Institute of Certified Public Accountants (AICPA).
- Audit-based framework focused on:
  - Confidentiality
  - Integrity
  - Availability
  - Privacy
- Commonly used by service providers handling sensitive customer data.

### Purpose of SOC 2

- Demonstrate that adequate security controls are in place.
- Provide assurance to:
  - Customers
  - Stakeholders
  - Business partners
  - Regulators

- Independent auditors evaluate control effectiveness.

### SOC 2 Audit Coverage

May include:

- Physical security
- Network security
- Encryption (data in transit and at rest)
- Backup and recovery
- Incident management
- Employee training and awareness

---

## SOC 2 Audit Process

1. Define Scope:
   - Identify systems, processes, and locations in scope.

2. Select Auditor:
   - Choose qualified and experienced independent auditor.

3. Plan Audit:
   - Define timeline, criteria, and scope.

4. Prepare:
   - Review and improve policies, controls, and documentation.
   - Address identified gaps.

5. Conduct Audit:
   - Auditor tests controls.
   - Includes interviews, documentation review, and control testing.

6. Receive Report:
   - Report outlines:
     - Control effectiveness
     - Deficiencies
     - Recommendations

---

## Key Takeaway

- IS Management ensures structured protection of information assets.
- ISO 27001 provides a formal ISMS framework based on risk management.
- SOC 2 provides third-party assurance of security controls for service organisations.
- Both strengthen security governance, trust, and regulatory compliance.

# What I Learned — Privacy & Data Protection Regulations (GDPR, PCI DSS)

- Privacy and data protection regulations protect **Personally Identifiable Information (PII)**.
- PII = any data that can directly or indirectly identify an individual.
- These regulations:
  - Protect personal data from misuse.
  - Promote responsible data handling.
  - Build public trust.
  - Ensure legal compliance.

---

## General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)

- Implemented by the European Union in May 2018.
- Applies to organisations that:
  - Operate in the EU, or
  - Process personal data of EU residents.
- Defines personal data as any information that can identify an individual directly or indirectly.

### Core Principles:
- Obtain prior consent before collecting personal data.
- Collect only necessary data (data minimisation).
- Implement adequate security measures to protect stored data.
- Inform individuals how their data is processed.

### Penalties for Non-Compliance:

- **Tier 1 (Severe Violations)**
  - Examples: unlawful data sharing, improper data collection.
  - Fine: up to 4% of annual global revenue or €20 million (whichever is higher).

- **Tier 2 (Less Severe Violations)**
  - Examples: failure to report data breaches.
  - Fine: up to 2% of annual global revenue or €10 million (whichever is higher).

### Key Impact:
- One of the strictest global privacy laws.
- Forces organisations to adopt transparent and secure data handling practices.

---

## Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)

- Focuses on protecting cardholder data.
- Created by major credit card brands:
  - Visa
  - MasterCard
  - American Express
- Applies to businesses handling payment card data.

### Core Requirements:
- Restrict access to cardholder data.
- Monitor and log access attempts.
- Implement security controls such as:
  - Encryption
  - Web application firewalls
- Prevent fraud and data theft in card transactions.

---

## Key Takeaway

- GDPR protects personal data broadly across sectors.
- PCI DSS specifically protects payment card information.
- Both enforce strict security controls to reduce data breaches and financial fraud.

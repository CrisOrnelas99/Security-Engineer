# TryHackMe – Risk Management Fundamentals | Notes & Key Concepts Learned

Before studying risk management, it is essential to clearly define key terms to avoid ambiguity.

---

## Threat

A threat is an intentional or accidental event that can compromise the security of an information system. It represents potential harm or danger to an individual, organisation, or system.

### Categories of Threats

### 1. Human-Made Threats
Caused by human activities or actions.

Examples:
- Terrorism
- Wars and conflicts
- Riots and civil unrest
- Cyberattacks
- Industrial accidents
- Arson

These threats can disrupt business operations, whether technical (cyberattacks) or non-technical (arson).

---

### 2. Technical Threats
Result from technological failures, malfunctions, or vulnerabilities.

Examples:
- Power outages
- Software failures
- Hardware failures
- Data breaches
- Network vulnerabilities
- Equipment malfunctions

Technical threats can interrupt business continuity if backup systems are not in place.

---

### 3. Natural Threats
Caused by environmental or natural events.

Examples:
- Earthquakes
- Floods

Natural threat analysis depends on geographic location and environmental exposure.

---

## Vulnerability

A vulnerability is a weakness in software, hardware, or networks that can be exploited by a threat.

Vulnerabilities may arise from:
- Software bugs
- Misconfigurations
- Outdated security controls

They can compromise:
- Confidentiality
- Integrity
- Availability

---

## Asset

An asset is a valuable resource (tangible or intangible) that an organisation relies on to achieve its objectives.

In information systems, assets include:

### Hardware
- Servers
- Workstations
- Routers
- Switches
- Firewalls

### Software
- Operating systems
- Applications
- Databases

### Data
- Customer records
- Financial data
- Intellectual property
- Employee information

### Documentation
- Manuals
- Policy documents

Assets are central to risk because they represent what must be protected.

---

## Risk

Risk is the probability of a threat exploiting a vulnerability in an asset and resulting in adverse business effects.

Risk can be understood as:

Risk = Threat × Vulnerability (impacting assets)

Risk represents:
- Potential loss
- Damage
- Business disruption
- Negative outcomes

In information systems, risk involves the interaction between:
- IT infrastructure
- Software applications
- Data
- Users

It affects the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of digital assets.

---

## Risk Management (RM)

Risk Management is the process of:

- Identifying risks
- Assessing risks
- Responding to risks
- Monitoring risks

It aims to maintain risk at acceptable levels.

Risk management includes:
- Evaluating likelihood and impact
- Implementing mitigation strategies
- Monitoring effectiveness of controls

---

## Risk Management Policy

A Risk Management Policy is a structured set of procedures designed to:

- Identify risks
- Assess risks
- Assign responsibilities
- Ensure compliance with laws and regulations
- Reduce the likelihood of adverse outcomes

---

## Information Systems Risk Management

Information Systems Risk Management focuses specifically on protecting computer systems from internal and external threats.

It includes:
- Identifying threats
- Assessing probability of occurrence
- Evaluating mitigation measures
- Allocating resources
- Monitoring system integrity

The objective is to safeguard organisational assets while maintaining operational continuity.
